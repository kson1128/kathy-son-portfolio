Genzi

Opportunity

We can all agree that music connects people regardless of their background, social status, and culture. As an electronic music fanatic, I have struggled to find new music and people to connect with. That was the idea that is now known as Genzi, a social media platform to share music and connect with other people with similar musical styles.
LEARN MORE

Motivation

As we started the project, one of our main focuses was to create a following on social media. Different approaches were taken into consideration including the design process, interactive videos, interviews, and building a start-up process. Within a few months, our videos reached 500,000+ accounts. As we continue to upload videos, our extrinsic motivation kept rising, reinforcing and validating the idea of Genzi.
LEARN MORE

The Overview

Role:

As the co-founder and UX | UI Designer, my role includes a bit of everything. During the first phase, I worked along with my co-founder to have a detailed market analysis of the business plan including key competitors, user's needs, and gaps and opportunities in the market. Together we brainstormed ideas and designs for the lo-fi version and wireframe of the application. As we shaped our idea, we started sharing a transparent journey on social media focusing on the process of building an application and engaging with our potential users.

The Discovery

Our Target Market:

The name Genzi was chosen to represent a generation that is not afraid to speak up, express themselves, and share their achievements and opinions with others online. As the name suggests, our primary target is Generation Z. As we continue to grow on social media, our analytics show a strong following from the age of 16 to 24 located in Spain.

Market Analysis:

Understanding our demographics and target market enabled us to understand how to proceed. We determined that our primary demographics would be Spain and Latam given there is no such application for this market. As we researched and experienced the competitors' apps, we created an empathy map. Information was gathered from different sources where most of the users participated including the competitors' apps, TikTok, Reddit, and Twitter.

The Process

Product Design:

During the lo-fi creation of the application, a big-picture storyboard was created. After understanding the market's needs, we focused on what showcasing what the user needs, their context, and why the app will be useful for them. This allowed us to understand the key features that are essential for the minimum viable product (MVP) version. A website landing page was created where potential users can register on the waiting list by submitting an email address. As the lo-fi version was determined, I proceed to create the hi-fi version of the app using tools such as Figma, Illustrator, and Webflow.

Content Creation:

Creating an audience for a social media platform that does not exist was going to be hard, or so we thought so. Our focus was to create videos on TikTok and Instagram and within a few months, our videos reached 500,000+ views, 32,000+ likes, and 300+ waiting list registrations. On TikTok, we focused on engaging with the audience but showing our journey and creating interactive videos. While on Instagram, posts and stories were mostly created. These videos and posts were mostly edited with Premiere Pro, Illustrator, and Canvas.

The Outcome

Ongoing Project:

As of September 2022, the hi-fi version of Genzi has been finished, we continue to grow our audience on social media, and we are currently raising capital to develop and launch the first version of the app. I am a firm believer that the need to create new experiences and bond with others is innate to every single one of us and Genzi will be there to make it happen.
